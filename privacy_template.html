Privacy & cookies

What this site collects, and your choice

This is a personal site. It doesn't sell anything, run ads, or build a profile of you. The only thing it measures is anonymous, aggregate traffic — and only if you agree to it.

The one tool: Google Analytics

The site uses Google Analytics 4 to understand roughly how many people visit and which pages they read. When enabled, it collects things like the pages you view, your approximate location (derived from your IP address, which Google does not log in full), your device and browser type, and the site that referred you. It does not see your name, email, or anything you type.

Cookies

Until you press Accept, Google Analytics runs in a cookieless mode and no analytics cookies are set. If you accept, it stores a couple of first-party cookies (_ga and _ga_<id>) used to tell returning visits apart. These expire up to two years after your last visit. The only other thing stored is your consent choice itself, kept in your browser's local storage so the banner doesn't ask again — that never leaves your device.

Why it's off by default

Under the GDPR, analytics cookies need your consent (Article 6(1)(a)) — so the default is denied, and nothing tracks you until you actively choose Accept. Declining is exactly as easy as accepting, and costs you nothing: the site works the same either way.

Changing your mind

You can switch at any time. Use the “Cookie settings” link in the footer of any page to reopen the banner and choose again. Choosing Decline stops any further analytics cookies; you can also clear cookies for this site in your browser to remove existing ones.

Who else is involved

Google acts as the data processor for the analytics described above; you can read Google's privacy policy for how they handle it. No other third parties, trackers, or advertising networks are used.
